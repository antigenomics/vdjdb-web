TSR: TCR Structural Repertoire
database of all TCR structures, containing CDR classification and epitope targeting.

This database is a comprehensive, curated collection of T cell receptor structures from the Protein Data Bank, analyzed for structure, sequence, and antigen recognition, as well as TCR germline gene sequences from IMGT and TCR sequencing data from various studies.

It was developed by the Pierce Laboratory at the Institute for Bioscience and Biotechnology Research of the University of Maryland.

TCRmodel
automated high resolution modeling of T cell receptors.

TCRmodel is a web server that models T cell receptor (TCR) structures from sequence, developed by the Pierce Lab at the University of Maryland Institute for Bioscience and Biotechnology Research.

TCRex
web tool for the prediction of TCR–epitope recognition

TCRex predicts TCR–epitope binding for human T cell receptors (TCRs) using TCR beta chain information, i.e. the CDR3 amino acid sequence and the corresponding V/J genes. It is based on random forest classifiers trained on epitope-specific TCR data collected from the manually curated catalogue of pathology-associated T cell receptor sequences (McPAS-TCR) and the VDJ database (VDJdb).

TCRex has been developed as a joint project between AUDACIS, biomina, and the Adrem Data Lab.

ATLAS
database of T cell receptor mutant affinities and structures.

The ATLAS (Altered TCR Ligand Affinities and Structures) database is a manually curated repository containing the binding affinities for wild-type and mutant T cell receptors (TCRs) and their antigens, peptides presented by the major histocompatibility complex (MHC). The database links experimentally measured binding affinities with the corresponding three dimensional structures for TCR-pMHC complexes.

IMGT
the immunogenetics database, containing germline gene information.

IMGT® is a high-quality integrated knowledge resource specialized in the immunoglobulins (IG) or antibodies, T cell receptors (TR), major histocompatibility (MH) of human and other vertebrate species, and in the immunoglobulin superfamily (IgSF), MH superfamily (MhSF) and related proteins of the immune system (RPI) of vertebrates and invertebrates.

External link
McPAS-TCR
database of pathology associated T cell receptor sequences.

McPAS-TCR is a manually curated catalogue of T cell receptor (TCR) sequences that were found in T cells associated with various pathological conditions in humans and in mice. It is meant to link TCR sequences to their antigen target or to the pathology and organ with which they are associated.

The database can be queried by disease condition, T cell type, tissue, epitope, source organism, MHC restriction, assay type and other criteria.

IEDB
immune epitope database and analysis resource.
The IEDB is a free resource, funded by a contract from the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases. It offers easy searching of experimental data characterizing antibody and T cell epitopes studied in humans, non-human primates, and other animal species. Epitopes involved in infectious disease, allergy, autoimmunity, and transplant are included.
